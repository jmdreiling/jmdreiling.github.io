Foundations of Resilience Engineering, a Dr. David Woods Lecture Series

June - August 2024
I worked for the Cognitive Systems Engineering Lab (CSEL) at The Ohio State University editing video lectures by Dr. David Woods to produce accessible introductory resilience engineering education materials. This included:

  • editing hours of footage
  • basic sound cleanup and color correction
  • creating flexible and novel graphics to aid understanding
  • animating key concepts
  • publishing approved videos to the CSEL YouTube Channel
    From there I built out a graphical navigation website to explore different concepts through the videos as well as two subpages for the CSEL home website. I developed a much deeper understanding of the field of Resilience Engineering (RE) along with my Adobe Suite Skills. I am deeply committed to producing accessible and educational RE content as I believe our systems need to be developed no longer with just basic fail-safes in mind, but with Resilience in the forefront of our design decisions.

Gamma-Ray Energy Tracking Array (GRETA)

October 2022 - June 2024
Throughout my third and fourth year at Ohio State, I worked on the calibration and filter optimization system for Berkeley labs' GRETA project under the guidance of Dr. Heather Crawford and Dr. Marco Salathe.

Because I am not an expert in low level nuclear physics, I spent much of my first year researching the parameter space of the GRETA detector and learning about signal filter shaping parameters and methods. Then in the summer of 2023, I moved out to Berkeley for a few months to participate in the US Department of Energy's Summer Undergraduate Laboratory Internship (SULI) program to build the initial optimization pipeline for GRETAâ€™s detectors. After my SULI experience wrapped up, I continued working remotely to refine the pipeline and expand it's capacity beyond just the crystal cores the initial system was based on. During this time I attended the 2023 Fall Meeting of APS DNP and JPS in Hawaii where I presented my pipeline work at the CEU poster session and visited Michigan State's Facility for Rare Isotope Beams in January 2024 to assist with an experiment using GRETINA, the predecessor to GRETA. I finalized my work in May 2024 and spent June writing extensive documentation for future users and programmers.

During this time I developed skills in:

  • Signal Processing
  • Basics of Low-Level Nuclear Physics
  • Becquerel Python Package
  • NLOpt Python Package
  • PyEPICS Python Package
  • Technical Writing
  • Detailed Documentation
